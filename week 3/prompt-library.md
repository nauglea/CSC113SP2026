## Learning & Studying

### 1. [Introduction to material]
**Prompt:** [Explain [the 4 types of shock] to me step by step as if I’m learning it for the first time. Use simple language, real-world examples, and analogies. Afterward, ask me 3 questions to check my understanding and explain the answers.
**Use this when:** [Being exposed to new content and want to find out what I know about the topic before doing a deep dive.]
**Works best with:** [Any but focus on using the AI generator that comes with the products the school is using to teach the material]

### 2. [Review for exam]
**Prompt:** [Help me review [the 4 types of shock] for an upcoming test. Identify the most important concepts, common mistakes students make, and likely exam questions. Then quiz me with a mix of easy, medium, and hard questions and give feedback on my answers.]

### 4. [Active Recall and Study materials]
**Prompt:**[Turn [attached notes] into a complete study pack: concise summary, bullet-point key takeaways, a flashcard set (term on one side, explanation/example on the other), and practice problems with step-by-step solutions.]
**Use this when:** [Trying to summarize large powerpoints or outlines to minimalistic notes and flash cards.]
**Works best with:** [Claude takes a little bit of time, but preferred over the information from Gemini. ]

## Evaluating & Improving

### 5. [Feedback and proofing]
**Prompt:**[Review my work below and evaluate it against [rubric / criteria / learning objectives]. Point out any errors, gaps, or unclear reasoning. For each issue, explain why it’s a problem and show how to fix it. Then give an overall quality score and specific next steps to improve.]
**Use this when:** [Making sure that my lectures align with student learning outcomes]
**Works best with:** [AI generator associated with the product used in the nursing program.]

### 6. [Revise and Improve]
**Prompt:**[Here is something I’ve already created: [paste work]. Suggest concrete improvements for clarity, accuracy, structure, and depth—without changing my original voice. Provide a revised version, followed by a brief explanation of what was improved and why.]
**Use this when:** [Previously made lectures and activities that need refreshing]
**Works best with:** [Any- cluade is preferred]
